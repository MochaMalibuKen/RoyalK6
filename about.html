About Royal’K6 Youth In Action

Empowering youth. Strengthening families. Transforming communities.

Our Story

Royal’K6 Youth In Action was founded by Kenya N. Washington and her husband Roy Washington, leaders rooted deeply in service, faith, and the belief that today’s youth are tomorrow’s change-makers. Their work grew from years of grassroots mentoring, community service, support for young people in difficult situations, and a powerful vision: to make every young person feel seen, supported, prepared, and uplifted.

Their mission caught the attention of the community and local media, including being featured in the WACH Carolina Champion spotlight. Today, Royal’K6 is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit (Tax ID: 87-0866832) focused on mentorship, skill building, leadership development, life readiness, and positive community impact.

Who We Are

Our Mission

To empower and inspire young individuals to become proactive leaders and compassionate citizens in their communities. We provide a nurturing platform for youth to develop their skills, explore passions, and engage in meaningful projects that promote social responsibility, innovation, and positive change.

Our Vision

To cultivate a generation of informed, resilient, and driven young individuals who will lead with integrity, embrace inclusivity, and transform their communities through creativity, service, and innovation.

Our Promise

We promise to provide a nurturing space where every young person feels supported, empowered, and inspired to reach their full potential. We commit to transparency, compassion, and meaningful engagement with both youth and their families.

Why We Serve

Royal’K6 was built from a shared belief that every child—no matter their background—deserves support, guidance, opportunity, and unconditional belief in their worth. Through mentorship, skill development, structured activities, and community service, we strive to break cycles, build confidence, and create pathways toward a brighter future.

We stand for hope. For safety. For emotional and spiritual growth. For discipline, respect, commitment, and love. Because when youth feel empowered, entire communities rise.
